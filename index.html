How I calculated the Impact Score

I didn't want to just count commits or lines of code ‚Äî those reward noise, not value. Instead I asked: what does it actually look like when an engineer is making the team move faster? Here's what I landed on:

√ó3
Shipping velocity ‚Äî I weighted merged PRs most heavily because at the end of the day, value is only delivered when code ships. An engineer who consistently closes PRs is unblocking the team and driving the product forward.
√ó2
Code reviews given ‚Äî I gave this a high weight because reviews are a direct multiplier on teammates' output. An engineer who reviews 20 PRs has effectively co-authored 20 pieces of work. This is often invisible in raw commit data.
√ó0.6
Codebase breadth ‚Äî I counted unique files changed (capped at 60) to reward engineers who work cross-cutting rather than staying in one corner. Broad touch signals architectural thinking and fewer siloes.
+up to 25
Lines contributed ‚Äî I included this as a soft signal of substance, not raw effort. I capped the contribution at 25 points so a single large refactor doesn't dominate the score over consistent delivery.
+6 bonus
Merge speed ‚Äî I added a small bonus for engineers who get PRs merged within 48 hours. Fast merge times suggest well-scoped, reviewable work ‚Äî and keep the rest of the team unblocked.
